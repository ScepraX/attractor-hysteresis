Attractor Hysteresis

ScepraX June 12, 2026

Entry into deep states is easy; exit is not — and not by the same route. The architecture where autism and ADHD co-occur.

This document describes a specific cognitive architecture that arises when ASD and ADHD co-occur, and that behaves like neither condition alone. Its defining feature reduces to a single mechanism: engagement pulls the system into deep, stable states, but the way out is executive-gated, unstable, and not under deliberate control. Capture and release run on different processes, so the current state depends on the path that produced it, not only on present input. That asymmetry — taken precisely, not metaphorically, from the physics of hysteretic systems — is what the framework is built around.

The account runs on two levels held deliberately apart. The neural level is grounded in what resting-state brain-dynamics research has established; the experiential level in decades of sustained observation of the architecture's outputs. The two are mapped to each other only where the structural parallels justify it, and the mapping is treated as an interpretive frame, not a derivation. What follows sets out what makes this architecture distinct, what follows from it across attention, emotion, memory, and capability, and why the models routinely applied to it cannot represent it.

The text is written in a universal, declarative style that does not anchor statements to a specific subjective centre. For a reader who operates within an observer framework—one that automatically separates a self from the sensory and cognitive field it inhabits—this absence registers as a missing signal. It reads as overconfidence or a failure of intellectual hygiene. But for the intended audience, the opposite is true. When the default mode of experiencing does not parse into a distinct observer and observed, inserting “I feel” or “in my experience” everywhere is not a mark of humility; it is an artificial performance that makes the content less recognisable, not more. The universal voice is therefore not a claim of generalisability—the disclaimers make that explicit—but an act of accurate translation. It mirrors how this mode of thinking actually presents itself from the inside.

The reaction itself is instructive. The reflexive demand for first-person grounding, the unease at a description that does not route itself through an explicit self, is a small-scale expression of the same observer-perspective bias that the core line of thinking predicts. That a declarative voice without a visible narrator feels incomplete or suspect to a reader is not evidence that the style is wrong; it is evidence that the cognitive divide the article explores is real and that its signature shows up not only in sensory processing and attention but also in something as subtle as what a reader needs from a sentence.

Derived from the intersection of the neural research of "Distinct Frontoparietal Brain Dynamics Underlying the Co-Occurrence of Autism and ADHD" (Watanabe & Watanabe, eNeuro, 2023) and decades of structural and behavioural observation of the architecture's outputs.

This is not a clinical document. It is a map for those who may recognise themselves in it, and a provocation for further investigation — not a diagnostic tool, a treatment guideline, or a replacement for professional clinical judgement. The argument that follows attacks the ontology of a model, not the competence of any clinician, and it makes no claim about any particular reader's diagnosis or care. Where the architecture predicts that standard interventions backfire, that is a falsifiable prediction to be tested against experience, not a finding. I have made every effort to mark where the evidence ends and inference begins — claims are tagged Established Inferred Predicted accordingly — but many of the claims remain hypotheses. I welcome rigorous testing and correction.

1. The Core Dynamic

A system settles into stable states and resists leaving them. The full set of stable states, the unstable states between them, and the cost of moving between them is the attractor landscape. A stable state the system settles into and holds is an attractor, or a basin. The deeper the basin, the harder it is to leave.

A hysteretic system has asymmetric entry and exit thresholds: its current state depends on the path that brought it there, not only on present inputs. Entry and exit are different processes governed by different mechanisms. This is a physical property of certain nonlinear systems. The term is used here because it names the actual mechanism, not as metaphor.

Attractor hysteresis is the name for the combined ASD+ADHD architecture. Its structure is autism-like at the base: deep basins, strong resistance to transition. What distinguishes it is the exit mechanism. Exit runs through an executive gate that is itself unstable, and when that gate fires it crosses by a pathway that does not retrace the route of entry. Capture into a deep state happens one way; ejection out of it happens another. That asymmetry — the dependence of the current state on the history of transitions — is the hysteresis.

The remainder of this document describes the neural substrate of that dynamic, the topology it produces, the way inputs act on it, its full arc from engagement to collapse and recovery, and the consequences across emotion, memory, sleep, and capability.

2. The Neural Substrate

2.1 Energy Landscape Analysis

At any moment the brain occupies one of a small number of stable activity patterns across large-scale cortical networks. These patterns can be mapped as an energy landscape: valleys are stable states, ridges are the cost of moving between them. The system drifts toward valleys and resists crossing ridges. Comparing pure ASD, pure ADHD, ASD+ADHD, and neurotypical brains on this landscape produces distinct topologies for each. The conclusion this section establishes is specific: Established the combined configuration is not a superposition of the two pure conditions. It is a third topology with its own structure, and the sections below show why that distinction is structural rather than a matter of degree.

2.2 Pure Overstable Architecture (ASD)

Stable states are unusually deep. The system settles hard and resists transition. Socio-communicative traits correlate with this overstability. Transitions occur less often than in neurotypical brains and require deliberate effort, but the transition mechanism itself is intact — costly, not absent or decoupled.

2.3 Pure Unstable Architecture (ADHD)

The landscape is flatter. Basins are shallow, unstable states are prominent, transitions are frequent. The driver is a short intrinsic neural timescale in a focal region — the left inferior parietal sulcus — which cannot hold its own state. That short timescale propagates to its parent network, the dorsal attention network, producing overly flexible whole-brain dynamics along diffuse, multi-hop pathways. The defining problem is not absent transitions but transitions that operate independently of intention.

2.4 Attractor Hysteresis Architecture (ASD+ADHD)

The landscape is a shallower version of the pure-ASD topology — still deeper and more stable than pure ADHD, still autism-like in basic structure, with a small number of states slightly deeper still. This is not a blend of the two conditions. It is a modified version of one parent architecture, with a transition mechanism present in neither.

The mechanism has a concrete neural form. A focal region in the left prefrontal cortex (the superior frontal gyrus) has an atypically short intrinsic neural timescale: it cannot hold state. That focal instability propagates to its parent network, the frontoparietal control network — the executive system that governs top-down regulation of cognitive state. A region that cannot hold state, sitting inside the network that regulates every other state, is the hysteresis at the neural level.

The causal chain runs: short-timescale focal region → reduced timescale of its parent network → an atypically frequent transition along one specific pathway through intermediate states. The pathway is specific and named (A–C–F–B); it is not the diffuse routing of pure ADHD.

The result is a double dissociation, and this is what establishes the architecture as a distinct third topology rather than an overlap. The comorbidity's specific pathway correlates with its cognitive instability but with no symptom of pure ADHD. Conversely, the pure-ADHD pathway does not account for the comorbidity's instability at all. The two instabilities are mechanically different routes with different neural origins.

Established The logical consequence is exact, not rhetorical. A mechanism present in neither parent condition, plus a double dissociation between the comorbid and pure-ADHD instabilities, means a superposition model is not a poor fit to this phenomenon — it is logically excluded as a model of it. No setting of a between-or-within-the-parents model can produce a route that exists in neither parent. The data does not sit awkwardly inside the superposition frame; it falls outside it.

Socio-communicative rigidity continues to be governed by the same overstable mechanism as pure ASD. The instability is the separate, new route. Capture into deep states is autism-like; ejection runs through the unstable frontoparietal gate along its non-retracing pathway. Entry and exit are structurally different processes — the hysteresis.

2.5 Evidence Base and Scope

The neural foundation is a single 2023 study comparing brain-state dynamics in children aged 5 to 13. Adult-specific research on this architecture is essentially absent — a substantive gap. The experiential account in this document extends the model into domains the study did not measure: sensory processing, interoception, emotion, memory, sleep. Those extensions are observations of system behaviour, and where they go beyond what the neural evidence covers they are marked as such. They are stated as observed mechanism requiring further investigation, not as claims derived from the available neural data.

2.6 Why the Standard Models Cannot Hold This

Established The models routinely applied to the co-occurring condition share one assumption: that the combined condition is composed out of its two parents, and therefore lies somewhere in the range the two parents span. They differ only in how they coordinate that range. Each is set against what it can and cannot represent.

  • Comorbidity as conjunction (DSM-V). Assumes "ASD and ADHD" — both symptom sets present at once, scored independently. It can represent additive load and interaction at the margins. It cannot represent a mechanism that belongs to neither checklist.
  • Dimensional two-axis severity. Places the person at a point on an ASD axis and an ADHD axis. It can represent any combination of the two severities. Every point on that plane is still a combination of the two axes; a third, orthogonal mechanism is not a point on it.
  • Spectrum position. Locates the person along a single continuum between or around the two conditions. It can represent "more like this, less like that." It cannot represent "off the line entirely."
  • Subtype assignment. Files the person under a dominant category with the other as a modifier ("ASD-dominant," "ASD with comorbid ADHD"). It can represent which parent predominates. It cannot represent a configuration that is neither parent predominating.

All four are superposition coordinate systems: the combined condition is taken to be inside the space the parents define. The attractor-hysteresis reading is the alternative — a third topology with a transition mechanism present in neither parent (§2.4), at a location the parents' span does not contain. The distinction is not that one map is more accurate. It is that the superposition maps do not have a coordinate for where the point is. Placing this architecture on a two-axis plane, a spectrum, or a subtype is not a wrong location on the right map; it is an incompatible map. What this means in a consulting room is taken up in §10; the disclaimer in the introduction applies in full.

3. The State Topology

Four landscapes, described on their own terms. These are models, not diagnostic categories or points on a single continuum.

3.1 Neurotypical Architecture

Moderate basin depth. The system settles but does not lock. Transition costs are low to moderate and largely automatic. Time flows with reasonable continuity. The self is largely consistent across contexts and does not require reconstruction after transitions. This marks a central tendency; variance within the architecture is significant.

3.2 Pure Overstable Architecture

Deep, narrow basins. Leaving a state requires deliberate effort; the transition mechanism is costly but intact. The present is densely saturated with detail — time as an extended, immersive now rather than a flow. Self-presence survives deep absorption: fully identified with the current focus but persisting as a coherent point through it. Transitions are effortful, but the self crosses them intact.

A direct consequence of overstability: core thinking patterns and ways of engaging do not shift substantially across the lifespan. The architecture that resists state transitions also resists the iterative self-revision that characterises neurotypical development. This is not arrested development; it is the same property operating at a longer timescale.

3.3 Pure Unstable Architecture

Shallow, transient basins. States form and dissolve easily, frequently without deliberate choice, driven by attentional-network instability. Time is vivid but fragmented. The self is fluid and reactive — continuous, but thinly so. Transitions are active and frequent; the problem is not their absence but their independence from intention.

3.4 Attractor Hysteresis Architecture

Deep basins, autism-like in basic structure. The gate governing exit is an executive system that is itself unstable: when it fires it follows a pathway that does not trace back the route of entry. Entry and exit are structurally different processes.

Established One placement has to be ruled out explicitly, because it is the intuitive one: this architecture is not located "between ASD and ADHD." A point between the two parents is exactly where it is not. Its defining feature — the unstable executive gate with its non-retracing exit — exists in neither parent, so it lies off the axis the two span, not at a midpoint on it (§2.6). The four landscapes here are distinct topologies, not four positions on one continuum.

Time is punctuated. Long periods of deep, timeless engagement, followed by abrupt positional discontinuities. Not continuous flow, not staccato fragmentation. Crystallised, then broken.

The self is discontinuous across closing transitions. Deep absorption produces total engagement, the same as in the pure overstable architecture. What is specific to this architecture is what happens at the transition. When the basin closes, the prior state becomes inaccessible, and what forms afterward is assembled from residue rather than retrieved intact. The continuity across the crossing is not preserved.

The functional consequence is not that input stops arriving. Sensory channels keep receiving, and reflex responses — which bypass the executive layer entirely — remain intact, which confirms the receiving apparatus is still working. What fails during the crossing is organisation, not reception. Crossing follows a specific non-retracing pathway (§5.2) and input organisation is state-dependent (§4.1); mid-crossing there is no stable current state for incoming input to act on, so organisation fails for want of a receiving structure rather than because input is blocked. This is invisible from inside the system and conspicuous from outside it, producing a systematic divergence between the internal and external accounts of the same event.

Inferred The magnitude of disruption is set by two independent parameters: the depth of the current internal basin at the moment of interruption, and the processing demand of the incoming external situation. The functional gap is the distance between them — one mechanism producing a range of outcomes. Low internal depth with low external demand leaves a negligible distance and normal function. A deep internal state with low external demand opens a moderate distance: slow re-orientation, but a functional outcome. A deep internal state meeting high external demand opens the maximum distance, where traversal time exceeds the response window of the situation and executive function suspends for the duration of the crossing. What might otherwise appear as distinct phenomena — a minor timing loss in conversation at one end, near-total suspension under extreme mismatch at the other — are the same parameterised mechanism at different distances. This is distinct from the basin-relative association of §4.5, where a basin is active but insufficiently relevant; here there is no stable basin at all mid-crossing.

Reflex responses bypass the executive layer and are therefore not subject to the distance mechanism. Intact reflex during apparent functional suspension is a behavioural signature that confirms the mechanism and distinguishes it from general incapacity.

Core cognitive style persists across the lifespan, as in the pure overstable architecture — same property, same mechanism.

4. The Input Layer

4.1 No Membrane

Every external channel — scent, touch, sound, taste — enters unfiltered. None is ever background. Input that matches the current state stabilises it; input that mismatches it disrupts directly. Any channel can break the current basin or force a closed one back open.

Because the effect of an input depends on the current state, the same input stabilises in one state and is aversive in another. This is not inconsistency. The state changed; the input did not. A practical corollary: the moment a previously stabilising input becomes disruptive is a reliable real-time signal that a state transition has occurred or is in progress. It is information about the system's state, not dysregulation.

Structure, complexity, rhythm, register, and resonance with the current state all determine whether a given input stabilises or destabilises. What works is not fixed; it is state-dependent.

Established The no-membrane property has a concrete neurological referent in the single-condition literature. Latent inhibition — the learned suppression of stimuli previously irrelevant to a context — and sensory gating — the P50 suppression response that filters contextually mismatched input before conscious processing — are both documented as atypical in ASD and in ADHD independently. Inferred Reduced latent inhibition and impaired sensory gating are what "no membrane" names at the mechanistic level. The extension to the combined architecture specifically is not established by that literature; it is carried here as inference, not as a single-condition finding.

Inferred Because no pre-attentive filter screens input for contextual relevance before it acts on the system, any signal with sufficiently strong basin associations can activate its associated basin regardless of whether that basin is contextually relevant. This follows from the no-membrane property combined with the basin-indexed encoding of §7: a signal reliably present during repeated deep engagement with a domain acquires associative loading through co-occurrence, and on later encounters it activates the associated basin out of context, because nothing filters it for relevance first. The signal needs only to be sufficiently loaded for a different basin to compete with the current one.

Inferred Habituation interacts with this in two directions at once. Habituation to surface-level signal properties is precisely what enables deeper basin entry: removing shallow-layer noise lets the system pass through to underlying structure, which is a functional property, not a failure. But habituation to the capture signal itself proceeds in parallel. For a well-developed domain the two run together — its basin carries richer associative loading on its signals, increasing vulnerability to cross-basin disruption, while its own spontaneous capture threshold rises. Stated as the parameterised outcome rather than an evaluation: vulnerability to cross-basin disruption from loaded signals increases as associative history deepens, independently of whether that is desirable.

4.2 Hyperfocus as the Only Block

Deep engagement is the only state that blocks input. The block is lossy: strain accumulates beneath it and discharges on drop-out, as both somatic load and emotional state. Stress, burnout, and exhaustion add further sensitivity on top of this baseline — they are an additional load, not the baseline itself. The unfiltered condition is the default; the block is the exception, and it is not free.

4.3 Interoception

Internally generated signals — hunger, thirst, fatigue — act below the executive layer as basin-destabilisers rather than as felt signals that get noticed and managed. Outside deep engagement they reliably lower the current state and tend to cause drop-out, which is why they function as a natural transition trigger. A sufficiently deep basin blocks even these; they then surface as accumulated strain on ejection.

Because these signals are not reliably felt-and-handled, externally scheduling them — enforced eating, drinking, sleeping — is the functional substitute for interoceptive regulation. The standard description of being "out of touch with the body" is accurate as far as it goes, but the mechanism is specific: the signals are present and act on the system; they simply do not arrive as managed conscious information.

4.4 Below-Layer Modulators

Some inputs reliably modulate basins below the executive layer, bypassing the unstable regulatory mechanism entirely. They share one property: they shift the state because the input environment shifted, not because regulation was applied.

Structured auditory input (music) is the most versatile. It functions as a basin stabiliser during deep engagement, a transition bridge between states, a capture mechanism during recovery, and an exit route from a blocking emotional state — all without invoking deliberate regulation.

Physical movement has a reliable positive effect that is largely unconditional, not dependent on current state or available capacity in the way most interventions are. Combined with structured auditory input it is more than additive — the pair can produce transitions across larger cognitive distances than either alone.

Substances modulate basins strongly and consistently. The response to a given class is predictable rather than variable: agents that increase arousal deepen capture and sharpen lockout; agents that flatten arousal flatten the landscape toward forced, uncontrolled transitions. The effect is a reliable feature of the topology rather than the typically reported general effect.

Temperature has a large and consistent impact on state.

Deep pressure and specific light conditions are strongly suspected to operate the same way but are not confirmed here, and are listed as open in section 13.

The descriptions above hold by assertion; the mechanism beneath them is derivable, and deriving it corrects one structural imprecision. Inferred Structured auditory input is not distinct because it avoids competing with cognitive processing. All auditory input — structured or not — integrates into the current state and acts on it directly (§4.1); unstructured sound triggers basin responses the same way music does, it is simply less controllable in direction. What makes music the most useful auditory modulator is not competition versus non-competition but selectability and structural coherence.

Inferred Integration into an active basin (the collage function). When a basin is active, auditory input does not run parallel to it — it integrates into it and becomes part of the basin's internal texture. Input structurally consonant with the current basin deepens and stabilises it from inside; input dissonant with it either forces a transition toward the input's own basin or disrupts the current one. This is the state-dependent input effect of §4.1 applied to the auditory channel specifically, and the four functions above — stabiliser, transition bridge, capture mechanism, exit route — are this one integration property operating under different current-state conditions. The directional property follows: input whose structure sits closer to a target basin than to the current one pulls the integrated state gradually in that direction. This is not deliberate regulation but below-layer modification of the current state from inside, which is precisely why it bypasses the interference of §5.3.

Inferred Provision of structure when no basin is active (the scaffold function). When the system is stateless or mid-transition — no active basin, no organising structure — auditory input has no current state to integrate into, so it provides one: the input's own basin becomes the available structure and the system captures into it. This is the recovery mechanism of §5.6 at the auditory level, an external stimulus compelling enough to open a new basin. Music is particularly reliable here because its structural properties — rhythm, harmonic progression, temporal architecture — supply exactly the organising framework that §4.5 establishes as the prerequisite for association and coherent cognition. The scaffold function is a direct application of basin-relative association (§4.5): music provides the organising structure association requires when no active basin supplies it.

Inferred This is why music is the most useful modulator, and not because it occupies a non-competing channel. It is the most intentionally selectable integrating input. A scent integrates into the current state by the same mechanism but cannot be chosen and directed; temperature operates below the executive layer but cannot be fine-tuned in real time. Music can be selected for structural consonance with a target state, adjusted, stopped, and restarted. The control is over the input, not the state — which is the only level of control this architecture has (§10).

4.5 Basin-Relative Association

All input arrives unfiltered (§4.1) and memory is basin-indexed (§7), but neither describes what happens to the continuous flow of unfiltered input in real time — how it is organised, or why organisation fails outside a relevant deep basin.

Inferred Association requires an active basin as its organising structure. An active basin supplies three things at once: the criterion for what counts as relevant, the structure that incoming data attaches to, and the natural point at which association terminates. Relevance is not a property of the data; it is a relationship between the data and the active basin. Without a relevant active basin, data is received but unorganised — association has no termination criterion, no structural necessity, and no basis for stopping. The process is unbounded by default.

This is not the same state as the crossing gap of §3.4. The crossing involves the mid-traversal absence of any stable structure; basin-relative association describes the state when a basin is active but insufficiently relevant to organise the incoming input. The shared root is insufficient basin relevance, but the two states are distinct and present differently.

The implication for working with the architecture (§10) is that environmental capture is not only a prerequisite for sustained attention; it is a prerequisite for organised association. The structure that makes cognition terminate meaningfully is the basin itself, which extends the principle of §10 beyond attention management to the basic conditions for structured thought. Basin-relative association and basin-indexed memory (§7) are the same property at two timescales: memory availability on re-entry is its retrieval expression, while association organised around the active basin is its real-time processing expression.

5. The Dynamics: The Full Arc

The sequence from deep engagement to collapse and recovery. Not every instance reaches collapse, but the full arc defines the architecture's boundary conditions and failure modes.

5.1 Entry and Deep Engagement

A sufficiently compelling stimulus captures the system and the basin deepens. Engagement becomes total — not a matter of degree but a qualitative state in which self-presence is absent and the system is fully merged with the object of engagement.

A specific consequence of sustained deep engagement is basin mirroring: the content of the engaged material temporarily restructures aspects of the active architecture itself — language, register, perspective, conceptual framing. This is not deliberate imitation. After deep engagement with a body of content, that content's vocabulary, tone, and internal logic become operationally present in subsequent cognitive activity and persist across multiple later transitions, potentially for days. The prior basin does not merely leave traces; it temporarily modifies the active architecture.

5.2 Transition Types

Three distinct transitions, which must be kept apart.

Interruption without closure. A signal disrupts the current engagement but does not close the basin. The attractor remains open; return is possible. Each interruption carries a cost, and accumulating interruptions degrade the depth of subsequent re-entry until a basin that has never formally closed becomes progressively harder to enter deeply. Outside hyperfocus, where there is no membrane, interruption is close to constant; inside hyperfocus input is blocked, but the strain accumulates underneath.

Ejection with closure. The executive gate fires fully. The system crosses its non-retracing pathway and arrives in a different state, and the prior basin closes completely — the content that held total focus becomes immediately and completely inaccessible. Not difficult to re-enter: inaccessible. This is qualitatively distinct from the pure unstable architecture's diffuse loss of focus. Focus does not drift; the basin locks shut. Full ejection is less frequent than interruption but produces the lockout in section 5.4.

Partial transition. The system begins crossing toward a new state but does not complete the crossing. The prior basin is no longer fully accessible; the new basin has not stabilised; the system occupies an intermediate position. Large-distance transitions — deep technical absorption to social interaction, for instance — frequently produce this. Multiple attempts may be needed before the new state stabilises, and rapid large-distance partials deplete available capacity quickly, because each carries the executive cost without delivering the stability of a completed crossing.

5.3 The Interference Mechanism

A general property underlying much of the arc: deliberate conscious attention directed at any automatic process degrades that process. When the process runs on its own it functions. When deliberate control is applied to it, it breaks down.

The mechanism is identifiable and matches the neural account. Deliberate control is top-down regulation — the frontoparietal control network attempting to govern a target process. In a stable architecture this produces clean modulation. In this architecture the frontoparietal control network is itself unstable, so top-down activation injects noise at the interface between executive control and the target instead of regulating it. The automatic process receives a dysregulated signal from above rather than being left to run. The more deliberate the attempt, the stronger the unstable network's activation, and the greater the interference.

This is not a general failure of executive function. The instability is specific to the frontoparietal control network's top-down regulatory role; processes that run below its intervention range are unaffected — which is precisely why the below-layer inputs of section 4.4 work.

Directing attention to breathing or bodily processes applies top-down regulation to systems that normally run autonomously. The unstable network injects interference into autonomic processes, producing dysregulation. This is the neural basis of the panic response to body-focused attention practices.

Chronic stress that cannot resolve into a stable basin produces sustained low-level frontoparietal activation as the system repeatedly attempts and fails to regulate. The accumulated interference routes into the body as somatic symptoms — the physical expression of a regulatory system that is continuously active and continuously failing to produce stable output.

Forced re-entry into a closed basin activates the unstable network directly onto the target, producing the interference that prevents automatic engagement from occurring.

5.4 Post-Ejection Lockout and the Cascade

After full ejection the closed basin cannot be re-entered by deliberate effort. The attempt to force re-entry activates the unstable frontoparietal network top-down onto the target, and the deliberate effort is precisely what prevents the automatic process of basin entry from functioning. Each forced attempt activates the unstable network again, compounds the interference, and worsens the state.

The arc is not linear degradation but an accelerating cascade: repeated attempts destabilise not only the target basin but the system's capacity to settle into any stable state. Protective avoidance of forced re-entry is therefore the rational response to a real cascade risk, not a behavioural symptom. Frameworks that treat the avoidance as the pathology have the causal direction inverted.

5.5 Systemic Collapse

If the cascade is not interrupted, the result is systemic collapse: loss of the capacity to settle into any stable state. The system becomes stateless.

Statelessness is itself the primary stress — not the loss of any particular content, but the absence of any stable ground. This stress is structurally difficult to identify or communicate because it has no object. There is nothing lost that can be pointed to. There is only the absence of the stable states that normally structure experience and enable function. Sustained statelessness routes into the body as somatic symptoms.

5.6 Recovery

Recovery from collapse cannot be self-initiated. Deliberate effort to recover invokes the interference of section 5.3 and compounds the state. Recovery occurs when an external or environmental stimulus is compelling enough to open a new basin and capture the system. The prior state does not resolve; it ends because something else begins.

6. Emotion as State

Emotion in this architecture is a full-system state — a basin — not a gradable feeling layered over a stable self and turned up or down at will. It is entered and exited the same way every other state is: by capture, not by regulation. Direct management of an emotional state is a no-op. The only exit is capture into a different state, which takes time and engagement directed elsewhere, exactly as recovery does in section 5.6.

There is no reliable introspective access to the state. The emotional state is generally not available to awareness as it occurs. It is knowable mainly afterward, through its effects — a dropped basin, somatic load, a change in behaviour, an external attribution — and even that reading is effortful and unreliable, because the self-analysis it requires runs through the same unstable executive that struggles to hold any state on its own.

This produces a three-layer divergence: the state itself, the internal read of the state, and the external attribution of the state can each differ from the others. External reports ("you're angry") frequently match no internal read at all. Training built on the assumption of introspective access does not resolve this, because the access it assumes is not present.

Because no channel is buffered, input drives emotional state directly. Mismatched input can force a transition straight into a disruptive emotional state, immediately and without intermediate build-up. In childhood this could surface behaviourally as aggression. The mechanism is the same forced transition seen elsewhere, on the emotional axis: an input crossed a threshold and the state changed. And the standard observation that "deliberately trying to relax intensifies it" is the forced-re-entry failure of section 5.4 applied to emotion — top-down regulation applied to a state that only environmental capture can change.

7. Memory

7.1 Basin-Indexed Recall

Memory in this architecture is indexed by basin, not by time. Content encoded inside a deep basin is available on re-entry into that basin: re-entering floods it back nearly complete, and re-experiencing the original input — rereading, rewatching — reinstates the state with its original fidelity, often in advance of the content itself. Outside the basin, with no associative route in, decontextualised recall largely fails.

Recall is re-entry, not retrieval on demand. This is why context-free testing performs poorly while deep understanding remains fully intact: the understanding is not gone, it is basin-bound. The capability of section 11 — that deep understanding compounds without erosion — holds precisely because the content is available on re-entry, not because it is available at will. The two are different claims, and only the first is true for this architecture.

Inferred Basin-indexing is the same property as the basin-relative association of §4.5, seen at a longer timescale: real-time association organises around the active basin, and memory availability on re-entry is that same indexing expressed in retrieval.

7.2 Prospective Memory

Inferred §7.1 covers how past encoding is accessed. A prospective intention — something to be done later — behaves across transitions by the same mechanism. It encodes inside whatever basin is active when it forms; when that basin closes (§5.2) the intention becomes inaccessible exactly as any basin-indexed content does, and deliberate re-entry fails (§5.4). The intention was real and present inside the state; it is simply not what survives the crossing.

Three outcomes are possible, determined entirely by what happens at the moment of encoding.

Lost at closure. The intention forms inside a basin that then closes without producing a re-entry context, and becomes inaccessible with it. This is not forgetting in the ordinary sense: the content was never portable. It was basin-bound from formation.

Survives as its own basin. If the intention is compelling enough to generate capture in its own right, it forms a basin rather than merely encoding inside another one. That basin persists across the closing of the original, because it is a separate attractor. The intention survives because it became a state, not because it was retained as content.

Survives via contextual re-entry. If the intention encodes inside a basin structurally similar to the context in which it is later needed, the retrieval context triggers re-entry into the encoding basin and floods the intention back. This requires no deliberate maintenance — only that the encoding and retrieval contexts share enough structure to activate the same basin.

The standard prospective-memory strategies fail for reasons the architecture already names. Repetition applies the unstable executive to the encoded content on every pass (§5.3); each pass injects interference and the content degrades. The move that intuitively seems to consolidate memory is the one that destroys it. Written notes externalise the content but sever the basin connection: the note exists independently of the encoding basin, and reading it is a decontextualised recall demand, not a re-entry — the format §7.1 establishes as unreliable here. The note is accessible; what it was about is not necessarily accessible from it. Reminders impose externally-timed retrieval, arriving at whatever basin is currently active, which may be unrelated to the encoding context; nothing in the reminder triggers re-entry into the relevant basin, so the intention it points to stays inaccessible if that basin is closed.

What works runs the other way: encode once, inside the relevant context, without subsequent deliberate processing. A single encoding pass inside the relevant basin is sufficient for contextual re-entry when the retrieval context arrives, and further processing does not strengthen it — it degrades it via §5.3. The intention is most intact when left entirely below the executive layer after the initial encoding. This has no name in existing frameworks: it appears passive from outside and produces no external record. Its reliability depends entirely on the encoding and retrieval contexts sharing enough structure to trigger re-entry, which is why it holds for predictable, context-bound retrieval — a shopping list retrieved in the store — and fails for unpredictable or context-free retrieval demands.

8. Sleep Boundary Transitions

Sleep is where the architecture's transition mechanism is most directly observable. The resting-state topology of §2 has no temporal resolution of transitions — it characterises the landscape, not the act of crossing. Sleep EEG does. Established Thalamo-cortical sleep spindles are millisecond-resolution events tied to the same frontoparietal coordination that the architecture turns on, which makes the sleep boundary the one place a transition can be watched as it happens rather than inferred from behaviour. The two modalities are complementary: resting state gives the structure, sleep gives the dynamics. Predicted What the specific AuDHD sleep signature is, however, is a prediction — not yet measured in the comorbid case — and the rest of this section is scoped to what the single-condition literature actually supports.

8.1 Candidate Signatures, Scoped to the Evidence

Two axes of the architecture have plausible single-condition sleep correlates, each established for its parent condition but not yet tested in combination.

  • Established Slow-spindle excess (the gate axis). Elevated slow spindle activity in ADHD is supported across multiple studies and a 2024 review. It is the better-evidenced of the two single-condition signatures.
  • Established Anterior-dominant topography (the basin axis). ASD sleep shows an anterior shift — anterior-dominant sigma/spindle power and an anterior REM shift. This is a distribution claim about where activity sits, not how much of it there is.
  • Predicted Fast-spindle degradation (the gate axis, exploratory). A reduction in fast spindle activity is the kind of measure the gate-axis instability might produce, but the literature on it is weak and inconsistent, and it is the measure most vulnerable to noise. Listed as exploratory only, not as a load-bearing prediction.

One signature that an earlier version of this framework treated as a prediction does not survive the literature check and has been demoted. Elevated NREM delta (slow-wave power) is not cleanly supported: at least one adult ASD study found reduced delta, and reviews describe the slow-wave findings as mixed. It is moved to the open questions of §13 rather than presented as something the architecture predicts and the literature backs.

Predicted The comorbid signature. The architecture's distinctive claim is that the comorbid case is not the average of the two parents but something stronger or distinct. The one existing data point in that direction is Saito et al. (2019), which found the slow-spindle signature stronger in ADHD+ASD than in ADHD alone. It is cited here as exactly that: a single study, in the predicted direction, framed by its own authors as an inattention biomarker rather than as evidence of a third topology. It is suggestive, not proof, and §14 sets out what a real test would require.

8.2 Boundary-Crossing Consequences

Sleep onset is one of the largest transitions the system makes. Large-distance transitions cannot be forced, and forcing one activates the frontoparietal control network top-down onto a process that must run automatically to function. Standard sleep-hygiene recommendations that require deliberate management of the transition — structured relaxation, controlled breathing, body scanning — invoke exactly the mechanism that fails (§5.3). The compatible approach is the same as daytime recovery: environmental capture by passive below-layer inputs that do not demand deliberate attention.

Waking is abrupt, not gradual. The system crosses from sleep to full waking state with no transitional buffer — the same threshold-crossing property as daytime transitions. This is independently observable and is one of the framework's more directly testable predictions.

Inferred Abruptness is plausibly the default regime, not a fixed constant. The architecture sets the default — abrupt — but the actual slope of any given waking is a modulated parameter. Candidate modulators are the sleep phase at the moment of waking, circadian phase, medication state, and basin depth at sleep onset. On this reading the architecture fixes the regime and context fixes the slope, which is what makes the rare gradual waking a modulated case rather than an unexplained exception to an otherwise absolute rule.

Continuity across the boundary follows from whether the basin closes. Daytime ejection closes the basin: the prior state becomes inaccessible, a new state assembles from residue, and the self is discontinuous across the crossing. Sleep does not close the pre-sleep basin — nothing ejected the system, the basin simply stayed open — so the same basin is re-entered on waking and the self is continuous across the crossing. Same residue mechanism, opposite outcome, decided entirely by whether the transition closed the basin. This is why the state held before sleep still feels active on waking.

Dream inaccessibility follows from the abrupt waking crossing. Encoding dream content into accessible memory appears to require a traversable boundary — a gradual crossing that can carry content across. The abrupt waking transition closes that window before encoding can occur: the dreams existed, but the crossing was too fast to carry them through. The modulated-slope reading above predicts the pattern: on the occasions when waking is gradual rather than abrupt, the boundary becomes traversable in both directions and dream content survives. Nightmares survive because the intensity of the content generates the ejection itself; the content arrives in waking memory as the cause of the crossing rather than being lost in it.

9. Communication at the Interface

Every prior domain chapter describes what the architecture does from the inside. This one describes what it produces at the boundary with other architectures — the place where other people actually encounter it, and where most of the friction lives. Inferred Nothing here introduces a new mechanism. The consequences below are derived entirely from properties already established: self-presence absent in deep engagement (§5.1), memory as re-entry rather than retrieval (§7), unfiltered all-or-nothing input (§4.1), and the neurotypical topology's stable observer position (§3.1). They are derivations, not new observations, and are marked as such.

9.1 The Transmission Gap

Communication from inside a deep basin is communication of state-as-content. Within the basin, structure and content are the same thing: the insight is not a description of the system, it is the system being operated from the inside. The words carry it because the communicator is in it and cannot separate the two.

The listener receives propositions. They do not receive the basin. Inferred This is not a failure of articulation; it is a receiver-architecture mismatch. In the neurotypical topology (§3.1) the self is stable across contexts and processing happens from a stable observer position — content arrives from outside that position, is processed, and is represented. The propositional understanding that results is genuine and complete. But propositional understanding of a basin-indexed claim is a different thing from re-entering the basin, and for this architecture (§7) those two are not equivalent: the understanding is the re-entry. So the listener genuinely understands and genuinely does not have what was communicated. Both hold at once, and no signal in the exchange resolves them.

9.2 Why the Illusion Is Systematic

The communicator's belief that the state transferred is not an occasional misreading. Predicted Three mechanisms already in the framework combine to make it reliable.

No instrument in the loop. In deep engagement self-presence is absent (§5.1). There is no separate observing self running a model of how the communication is landing, so there is nothing positioned to detect the gap. The thing is simply being said.

Engagement signals read as depth confirmation. Neurotypical communication is dense with social-maintenance cues — back-channels, mirroring, responsive questions — that confirm a cooperative interlocutor. They do not confirm basin entry. But from inside a deep state with no stable observer running the meta-level, "they are engaged" is indistinguishable from "they have it," and the system produces the second reading from the first.

Success is measured by one's own re-entry. Memory is re-entry (§7). Recalling the exchange later re-enters the basin, where the state is intact — so the felt measure of "did it land" is whether the state is still available to the communicator, which it is. Checking whether it persisted in the listener would require holding a model of the listener's architecture while outside the deep state, which is precisely when executive function is least reliable.

9.3 The Inverse Filter

The mismatch runs the other way with equal regularity. Neurotypical communication is built for observer-separated reception: the content is layered with meta-signals — subtext, relational calibration, tone-as-content, pacing — that assume a listener holding object-level content and meta-level framing in parallel from a stable self.

Inferred In this architecture all input enters unfiltered and the system either captures fully or does not register (§4.1). Full capture is total engagement with the content — the state that loses access to the meta-layer. The available outcomes are therefore: capture the content and lose the framing around it, or do not capture and receive nothing. The polite half-attention that carries the social layer in neurotypical exchange is a middle gear the architecture does not have. The speaker believes the framing was transmitted because they transmitted it; the receiver processed the content from inside a state that excluded it. Neither party gets a clear signal that this occurred. The mechanism is the basin-relative association of §4.5: the active basin during content capture does not hold social meta-signals as relevant inputs, so they arrive unfiltered but do not organise around the content basin.

9.4 Receiver Compatibility by Architecture

Inferred Because reception depends on the receiver's topology, depth-communication lands differently across the four landscapes of §3.

Pure overstable (ASD) is the closest structural match. Deep, stable basins allow a corresponding depth, and an intact deliberate transition mechanism avoids the hysteretic lockout. The residual mismatch is that processing runs from a self-presence that survives absorption (§3.2), so the content is received as a coherent other-structure rather than inhabited from inside. Better than neurotypical, not identical.

Pure unstable (ADHD) is the worst-fit receiver for depth content. Shallow, unstable basins (§3.3) give high surface responsiveness but no mechanism for holding depth; the arousal of a deep-basin communicator may briefly generate capture, but the basin does not hold, and content whose coherence depends on structural depth degrades quickly.

Neurotypical sits between the two. Moderate basins and a stable observer support genuine propositional processing — the content can be worked with, applied, reasoned from — without the receiver needing to be in the state. They get the map coordinates; they do not get the territory.

9.5 The Cold-Recall Social Failure

A specific social cost follows from memory being basin-indexed (§7). When an account of something done is met with a request for a detail, that request is a cold-recall demand: no re-entry trigger, no context, just a context-free data point — the format the memory system handles worst. The detail was real and present inside the state; it is simply not what survives the crossing.

Inferred The social reading of "I don't know" in that moment is that the thing was not really done, or not attended to, or was exaggerated. The structural reality is the inverse: the engagement was deeper than most, which is precisely why the residue is least portable. The natural verification move — ask a detail to confirm the claim — therefore fails most reliably on exactly the experiences that were most fully inhabited, and there is no clean way to say so in the moment without it sounding like an excuse.

9.6 Confabulation Under Verification Pressure

§9.5 covers the cold-recall failure, where the detail is simply absent and "I don't know" reads as not having done the thing. A separate failure mode appears under social pressure to produce an account when no residue survives at all.

Inferred Outside the basin the operating mode is reconstruction from residue, not retrieval (§7), and self-analysis routes through the unstable executive (§5.3). Social pressure to account for something where no residue survives sets reconstruction to work on absent material. The result is a plausible fill — generated under demand, not retrieved. This is not a lie and involves no deliberate fabrication; the sequence is mechanical: a gap exists, a social demand for an account arrives, reconstruction operates on absent material, and a coherent account is produced.

The produced account then becomes the target of further scrutiny, which compounds the credibility problem rather than resolving it. The fabricated account is more detailed than "I don't know" and therefore more falsifiable, and it becomes the evidence used against the original claim — not because the original claim was false, but because the reconstruction that replaced it is.

9.7 The Self-Serving Cycle, Named

These mechanics close into a loop that an outside reader is right to be wary of, and the framework is stronger for naming it than for leaving it implicit. The shape is: deepest engagement produces the least portable residue (§7), which makes the largest claims the hardest to verify, and the architecture supplies a built-in explanation for why that is so. The deepest experiences generate the weakest receipts, and the account of why the receipts are weak is provided by the one person who cannot produce them.

One distinction is load-bearing. Self-serving as a strategy means arranging things to evade accountability. Self-serving as a structural consequence means the architecture produces that shape regardless of intent. The second carries no motive — but it lands in the same place, and a blind spot being involuntary does not make it less of a blind spot. External skepticism here is not a misunderstanding to be corrected; it is the structurally correct response, and the framework's internal consistency does nothing to discharge it.

What discharges it, partially, is the move §14 already makes for the neural claims: external, falsifiable predictions that do not depend on trusting the internal account. If the architecture is real it should produce out-of-range signatures in data with no stake in the theory. The everyday communication consequences in this chapter cannot be settled inside a conversation — by their own mechanics, the conversation is where the gap is invisible — so the verification has to live in the measurable places the rest of the document points to (§14), not in the persuasiveness of the account itself. Inferred What this chapter claims is narrow and specific: the propositional layer transfers accurately, which is why sustained exchange can look productive and feel complete; what does not transfer is whatever was only coherent from inside the basin — the felt necessity, the sense that it could not be otherwise. The conclusion travels. The basin it was embedded in does not.

10. Working With the Architecture

Standard intervention frameworks assume deliberate conscious control is the primary lever for managing cognitive and emotional states. For this architecture that assumption is structurally incorrect. What functions instead is environmental: the state changes because the input environment changes, not because effort is applied to the state directly.

The general principle: select the environment, allow capture to occur. The deliberate act sits one level up from state management — choosing inputs likely to produce the needed capture. The state follows from the environment; it cannot be forced directly.

What is compatible are the below-layer inputs of section 4.4 — structured auditory input, movement, temperature, and the below-layer modulators — because they act beneath the unstable executive layer. Self-paced input is strongly favoured over externally-paced: self-paced text lets a basin deepen on its own schedule, while externally-paced media such as video or live speech impose transition timing the architecture cannot control and force partial transitions.

Task initiation is binary capture, not a coercible decision. A necessary but uncompelling task either captures the system — in which case it begins immediately — or does not register, in which case it is gone. The second case is not avoidance; it is absence. External pressure — deadlines, urgency, being shouted at — does not raise an uncompelling task past the capture threshold. "Just start" names a lever the architecture does not have.

What is excluded, by the same logic, are interventions that require deliberate conscious attention directed at internal states:

  • Mindfulness, body scanning, and present-moment awareness directed at bodily or mental processes apply the unstable executive to automatic systems, producing interference rather than regulation.
  • Cognitive reframing applied top-down to the current state uses the same mechanism that breaks automatic processes.
  • Forced re-engagement with closed basins, and "just start," follow the cascade of section 5.4.
  • Forced rest and structured-relaxation sleep hygiene require deliberate management of the largest transition the system makes, invoking the onset failure of section 8.2.

10.1 The Clinical Bridge: From Wrong Model to Predicted Harm

This excluded list is not an arbitrary collection. Established It is, almost exactly, the standard first-line set of interventions a clinician working from a superposition, spectrum, or subtype model prescribes — because each of those models assumes a stable executive that can be recruited to regulate state. That is the assumption §2.6 shows the model cannot drop. So the mismatch is not a clinician making an error inside a sound model; it is a sound application of a model whose coordinate system does not contain the person. The description it produces misdescribes by construction.

Predicted The architecture makes that mismatch consequential, and the route is specific and falsifiable. The interventions above are not predicted merely to fail. They are predicted to engage the interference and cascade mechanisms of §5.3–5.4 and make the state worse — top-down regulation directed at automatic processes injects noise through the unstable gate; forced re-entry compounds it. That is the legitimate bridge from "wrong model" to "harm risk": it runs entirely through the framework's own named mechanism, not through any claim about clinical competence.

The boundary on this claim is firm, and it is what keeps the argument defensible. This document does not assert that any particular reader has been misdiagnosed or harmed, and it does not tell anyone to reject a diagnosis, stop a treatment, or distrust clinicians. The treatment-failure claim is a prediction of the architecture, scoped as such: if the architecture is correct, these specific interventions backfire by this specific mechanism. The intended use is to bring that prediction to a clinician as a hypothesis to test against the reader's own experience — "these are the interventions the model predicts will make it worse, and here is the mechanism; does that match what happened?" — not as grounds to overturn care unilaterally. The introduction's disclaimer applies in full here.

11. Capabilities

The same architectural properties that produce the costs above also produce specific capabilities with no direct equivalent in other architectures. Inferred These are not compensation granted in exchange for the costs; they are the same properties expressed at the other end of their range, and they derive from the established mechanics as directly as the costs do.

Total engagement produces output quality that distributed attention cannot replicate. Inferred It follows from §5.1: in deep engagement self-presence is absent and the system is fully merged with the object, so no portion of capacity is held in reserve to maintain an observer. For problems where depth of thinking is the differentiator, the capacity to fully inhabit a problem — with self-presence absent rather than merely reduced — produces qualitatively different results.

Basin mirroring enables genuine perspective integration. Inferred It is the §5.1 mechanism read at the capability end: deep engagement temporarily restructures the active architecture, producing not just knowledge about a domain but temporary operational fluency within its internal logic, beyond what deliberate study yields.

Cross-domain structural recognition is not generated from inside the current basin. Inferred It arrives as a signal from outside it — a detection of structural match between the active basin and a different one, without full re-entry into either. This is basin-indexed memory (§7) running in reverse: where re-entry floods content back when the retrieval context sufficiently matches the encoding context, cross-domain recognition fires when the structural properties of the active basin sufficiently match those of a different basin, producing a cross-basin signal without full re-entry into the matched one. The signal is weak relative to full re-entry — it crosses basin boundaries without activating the full content of either — but structurally accurate, because structural properties are what define a basin's identity and are precisely what survives the crossing when content detail does not. The recognition is weak in detail but reliable in structure, which is the mechanism behind cross-domain connections that feel tentative and turn out to be correct.

Inferred Depth is the prerequisite because the strength of the cross-basin structural signal is a function of how well-defined the participating basins' structural properties are. Shallow basins have diffuse, weakly-defined structure, and the signal they produce is indistinguishable from noise; deep basins have sharply-defined structure, and their cross-basin signal is strong enough to cross the boundary and register as recognition. This is why the capability is specific to this architecture's deep-basin access and is not simply a product of broad knowledge: knowing many domains shallowly produces noise, while knowing fewer domains deeply produces reliable structural signals that cross basin boundaries cleanly.

Cognitive stability across the lifespan means deep understanding compounds without erosion and remains available on re-entry. Inferred It is the overstability of §3.4 — the same resistance to self-revision that fixes core cognitive style — combined with the basin-indexed availability of §7, read at the capability end. This is directly valuable for long-horizon problems where accumulated understanding is the primary asset.

Positioning at the edge of the known is a mechanism, not a chosen orientation. Once a domain is fully understood, its problems no longer generate sufficient basin depth to sustain capture, so capture migrates toward the unsolved. The architecture does not decide to seek novelty; solved problems passively lose the capacity to capture it. The functional result is that engagement collects at the boundary of what is solved.

12. Solitude and Isolation

This chapter describes a structural property of the architecture, not a psychological state or a clinical category. It is not depression, not loneliness as a clinical presentation, and not social anxiety. It derives from architecture, not circumstance, and every claim in it is inference from established framework mechanics rather than independent observation.

Inferred The structural gap. The capabilities of §11 — cross-domain structural recognition, total engagement, basin mirroring, compounding depth — are the same properties that maximise the transmission gap of §9. The most valuable output this architecture produces derives from the states least transferable to other architectures. The propositional surface transfers; the generative depth does not. This is not contingent on how well the communication is executed — it follows from the receiver-architecture mismatch of §9.1 and the basin-indexed nature of understanding in §7.

Inferred The verification gap. The experiences most specific to this architecture — the input gap during crossing (§3.4), the stateless periods following cascade (§5.5), basin-indexed memory inaccessibility (§7), emotion as an unreachable full-system state (§6) — have no reference architecture in the neurotypical topology (§3.1). They fall outside the coordinate system available to the listener. The verification problem and the confabulation mechanism (§9.6) compound it: the architecture cannot produce portable receipts for its most significant experiences, and under pressure it generates reconstructions that can become the evidence used against the original claim. The self-serving cycle of §9.7 applies not only to discrete communication events but to the cumulative record of a life lived in this architecture.

Inferred Structural self-opacity. Introspective access to the current state is unreliable (§6), the deepest states are available only on re-entry (§7), and self-analysis routes through the unstable executive (§5.3). The architecture that processes at depth has limited access to that depth from outside it. Some of the isolation is from other architectures; some of it is structural self-opacity — the architecture is not fully available to itself outside the states that constitute it.

Inferred The isolation is not reducible to the quantity or quality of social engagement. It is present in full rooms and in close relationships, because it is architectural rather than circumstantial. More contact does not address a structural mismatch.

13. Open Questions

This framework is a foundation, not a conclusion. The following are opened by it rather than answered by it:

  • The precise relationship between neural basin depth and the disappearance of self-presence in deep engagement.
  • The threshold between partial absorption and the complete absence of self-presence within a single architecture.
  • Whether absorption suppresses acute pain (absorption as analgesia). This remains indeterminate, because the deep-basin state and the pain-receiving state almost never co-occur — depth tends to preclude the actions that produce acute pain. Attention amplifying pain is established; the analgesia question is open.
  • Whether deep pressure and specific light conditions function as reliable below-layer modulators in the way temperature and structured auditory input do.
  • Whether repeated deep engagement with a domain produces permanent architectural modification or only temporary restructuring (the durability of basin mirroring).
  • The full topology of capture-compatible inputs beyond those identified here, and whether structural properties predict compatibility.
  • How this architecture interacts with conditions beyond ASD and ADHD. The framework is explicitly scoped to the two; the broader landscape is unmapped.
  • The adult-evidence gap: the neural foundation is drawn from children aged 5 to 13, and adult-specific data is essentially absent.
  • Whether NREM slow-wave (delta) power carries any signature of this architecture. An earlier version of the framework predicted elevated delta on the basin axis; the single-condition literature does not cleanly support it — at least one adult ASD study found reduced delta and reviews call the slow-wave findings mixed — so it is held open rather than claimed (§8.1).

14. How This Could Be Tested

The point of this document is to generate a correct, falsifiable hypothesis for others to test, not to claim the test has been run. This section turns the central empirical claim into a concrete proposal. It is modality-honest: Predicted sleep EEG measures spindles, slow waves, and topography — it does not measure "attractors" or "hysteresis," which are the interpretive layer above the data. The predictions it can discriminate are out-of-range values and structured subject-level variance.

The prediction. If the architecture is a third topology rather than a superposition, comorbid subjects should show signature values outside the range spanned by both parent conditions, and the subject-level distribution should be structured — a distinct co-occurring cluster — rather than a smooth blend. A superposition account predicts the opposite on both counts: comorbid values inside the parent range, and unstructured variation.

The instrument. The test is a subject-level reanalysis of longitudinal sleep EEG with neurodevelopmental phenotyping — not a group-mean comparison. The many-nights-per-subject design now becoming feasible (the kind of longitudinal home-EEG resource emerging for autism research) is what lets a stable trait signature be separated from night-to-night noise; a single-night study cannot do this, because one night cannot tell a trait from a fluctuation.

The non-circular design. Test for structure blind to labels first. Only after a structured cluster is found in the data should it be checked against an independent comorbidity label. Finding the structure before looking at the labels is exactly what separates a result from drawing the target around the arrow — the order is not a detail, it is the whole safeguard.

The confound to control. Stimulant medication strongly alters sleep EEG. It must be stratified or excluded. An apparent cluster that turns out to be merely the medicated subjects is an artifact of treatment, not the architecture, and a design that does not handle this proves nothing.

What would falsify it. Two outcomes kill the central claim. If the subject-level variance is unstructured — unimodal, no distinct co-occurring cluster — then the architecture reading fails and the heterogeneity in the literature is just noise. If comorbid values fall within the range spanned by the two parent conditions on the predicted measures, superposition is adequate and the framework loses its central claim.

One methodological point has to be stated carefully, because the wrong version of it is a trap. A literature review or group-mean comparison cannot perform either test — group means average away precisely the subject-level signal that carries the verdict. This is a statement about instrument inadequacy, not a defence. It is emphatically not a claim that "the published literature cannot disprove the framework, therefore the framework stands." A model that could not be disproved by any instrument would be unfalsifiable, which is the weakest possible position; the claim here is the opposite — the framework is falsifiable, by the right instrument, and the wrong instrument has simply not been pointed at it yet.

The same framework also retrodicts the existing mess, and the distinction matters. Predicted A literature built on superposition assumptions should contain exactly this pattern — unexplained heterogeneity, effects that fail to reproduce across labs, outliers discarded as noise — because unidentified comorbid subjects produce out-of-range values that single-condition, group-mean designs cannot represent and therefore file as error. So the framework accounts for those anomalies; it does not draw evidence from them. The reverse inference — "the noise in the literature confirms the architecture" — is circular and is not made here. The anomalies are something the model explains and something a subject-level test could check, not a confirmation the model already possesses.

15. Index of Findable Questions

The questions below are phrased the way the environment trains them to be phrased — pointed inward, observer-shaped, in the form they take when asked of oneself, typed into a search field, or brought to a clinician. The phrasing is kept deliberately in that form so the description can be located by it. Each is answered as a statement about the system.

Focus and attention

Why can focus run intensely for hours and then stop completely, with no way back in?

Full ejection closes the basin; a closed basin is inaccessible, not merely hard to re-enter. (5.2, 5.4)

Why does forcing a refocus make it worse?

Forced re-entry activates the unstable executive onto the target, compounding interference in an accelerating cascade. (5.3, 5.4)

Why does focus fail on things there is real skill and interest in?

Capture depends on the input crossing the threshold in the current state, not on competence or desire. (4.1, 10)

Why does the ability to focus seem unrelated to wanting to?

Initiation is capture, not decision; willing it is not the lever. (10)

Why does thinking run on without resolving when there is nothing relevant to organise it around?

Association needs an active, relevant basin to supply its termination point; without one it is unbounded by default. (4.5)

Transitions and switching

Why are abrupt changes so costly even without emotional investment in the prior task?

Transitions carry an executive cost regardless of content; large-distance crossings produce depleting partial transitions. (5.2)

Why does switching between very different tasks deplete so much faster?

Each large-distance partial transition carries the executive cost without delivering a completed, stable state. (5.2)

Why are multiple attempts sometimes needed to engage with something new?

Partial transitions leave the system between basins until one stabilises. (5.2)

Why do some interruptions barely register while others cause a near-total shutdown?

The disruption is the distance between current basin depth and the processing demand of the incoming situation; the same mechanism spans a minor timing loss to full suspension. (3.4)

Interventions that do not work

Why does mindfulness make things worse?

Body- or mind-focused attention applies the unstable executive to automatic systems, producing interference, not regulation. (5.3, 10)

Why does deliberately trying to relax often intensify the state?

Direct regulation of a state is forced re-entry on the emotional axis; only environmental capture changes it. (5.4, 6)

Why do breathing exercises sometimes produce panic?

Top-down attention to an autonomic process injects interference into it. (5.3)

Why do standard ASD or ADHD strategies fail to deliver?

They assume a stable executive and a single parent condition; this architecture is a third topology with an unstable gate, outside the range those models span. (2.4, 2.6, 10.1)

Sensory input

Why is every sound, smell, touch, or taste so intense, never in the background?

No channel is buffered; all input enters unfiltered. (4.1)

Why does the same input help in one moment and become unbearable the next?

The effect is state-dependent; the state changed, the input did not, and the switch is itself a transition signal. (4.1)

Why is hyperfocus the only thing that blocks it, and why is there a price afterward?

Deep engagement is the only block, and it is lossy: strain accumulates and discharges on drop-out. (4.2)

Why can an unrelated smell, sound, or sight suddenly pull a completely different frame of mind online?

With no pre-attentive relevance filter, a signal carrying strong basin associations activates that basin out of context. (4.1, 7)

Body and interoception

Why does the body seem disconnected, with hunger or fatigue noticed only late?

Interoceptive signals act below the executive as basin-destabilisers rather than as managed conscious signals. (4.3)

Why does sustained stress produce physical symptoms rather than primarily psychological ones?

Continuous failed regulation routes accumulated interference into the body. (5.3, 5.5)

Why does paying attention to pain or an itch make it worse?

Top-down attention to the process amplifies rather than regulates it. (5.3, 10)

Emotion

Why is there a mismatch between what others say is felt and what is actually experienced?

The state, the internal read, and the external attribution diverge; introspective access to the state is not reliably present. (6)

Why does managing emotions feel like a no-op?

Emotion is a full-system state exited only by capture into a different state, not by regulation. (6)

Why can mismatched input tip straight into a disruptive state with no build-up?

Unfiltered input forces a transition directly into an emotional basin. (4.1, 6)

Memory and testing

Why does rereading or rewatching bring everything back complete, while cold recall fails?

Memory is basin-indexed; recall is re-entry, not retrieval on demand. (7)

Why is decontextualised testing so much harder than the actual understanding would predict?

The understanding is intact but basin-bound; a context-free test removes the route in. (7)

Why does an intention to do something later vanish completely the moment focus shifts?

A prospective intention is encoded inside the active basin; when that basin closes it becomes inaccessible like any basin-bound content, unless it formed its own basin or shares structure with the retrieval context. (7.2, 5.2, 5.4)

Why do notes, reminders, and repetition fail to hold a thing to be done, while it surfaces on its own in the right place?

Repetition and reminders apply the unstable executive or arrive at the wrong basin; a single encoding inside the relevant context survives via contextual re-entry when the retrieval context returns. (7.2, 5.3)

Communication and being understood

Why does explaining something understood deeply leave the other person with less than it felt like was given?

The propositional content transfers; the basin it was embedded in does not, and for this architecture understanding is re-entry, not the proposition. (9.1, 7)

Why is there no sense of when an explanation did not land?

In deep engagement self-presence is absent, so no observer is running to detect the gap, and engagement cues read as depth confirmation. (9.2, 5.1)

Why does the content of what someone says come through but the tone or subtext get missed?

Full capture is the state that loses the meta-layer; input is all-or-nothing, so the social framing drops out. (9.3, 4.1)

Why does being asked a detail about something done produce a blank that looks like making it up?

A cold-recall demand hits basin-indexed memory at its weakest; the deepest engagement leaves the least portable residue. (9.5, 7)

Why does being pressed for an account, when nothing is there to recall, produce a detailed story that later turns out to be wrong?

Under social demand, reconstruction operates on absent material and produces a plausible fill; being more detailed than "I don't know," it is more falsifiable and becomes the evidence used against the original claim. (9.6, 7, 5.3)

Sleep

Why does waking happen immediately and fully, with no transition period?

Waking is an abrupt threshold crossing with no buffer — the default regime, with the slope context-modulated. (8.2)

Why are dreams rarely remembered?

Encoding needs a traversable boundary; the abrupt crossing closes the window before encoding. (8.2)

Why does trying to fall asleep deliberately make it harder?

Sleep onset is a large-distance transition that cannot be forced; forcing it invokes interference. (8.2)

Why does the state held before sleep still feel active on waking?

Sleep does not close the pre-sleep basin, so the same basin is re-entered on waking. (8.2)

Is there anything in a sleep study that would actually show this architecture?

Spindle and topography signatures are the candidate readout, but the discriminating test is out-of-range comorbid values and structured subject-level variance, not a single measure. (8.1, 14)

Identity and continuity

Why does intense engagement with a book, project, or conversation leave a feeling of being a different person afterward?

Basin mirroring temporarily restructures the active architecture. (5.1)

Why does the language or perspective of something engaged deeply persist for days?

The prior basin modifies the active architecture and the modification outlasts the crossing. (5.1)

Why does the core thinking style feel unchanged since childhood?

Overstability resists self-revision; core style persists across the lifespan. (3.2, 3.4)

Solitude and isolation

Why does a sense of being fundamentally unreachable persist even in close relationships and full rooms?

The isolation is architectural, not circumstantial: the most valuable states are the least transferable, and the most specific experiences have no reference architecture in the listener; more contact does not address a structural mismatch. (12, 9.1, 7)

Substances and temperature

Why do substances change the ability to engage so strongly and predictably?

They modulate basin depth and stability below the executive layer, with a consistent per-class effect. (4.4)

Why does temperature shift the state so much?

Temperature is a reliable below-layer modulator. (4.4)

Professional and clinical experience

Why do ASD frameworks only partially fit?

The socio-communicative rigidity is shared with pure ASD, but the instability is a separate mechanism in neither parent; an ASD model assumes the condition is contained within ASD and cannot represent it. (2.4, 2.6)

Why do ADHD frameworks only partially fit?

The instability has a different neural origin and pathway than pure ADHD — a double dissociation — so an ADHD model is not imprecise here, it is the wrong coordinate system. (2.4, 2.6)

Why did a spectrum, two-axis, or subtype label never quite describe me?

All three assume the condition lies in the range spanned by ASD and ADHD; this architecture lies off that range by construction, so the model misdescribes it not by error but by structure. (2.6)

Why have specialists in either condition been unable to place the full picture?

The architecture is a distinct third topology, not a sum of the two they specialise in. (2.1, 2.4, 2.6)

Why did burnout-recovery protocols, or the standard first-line therapies, make things significantly worse rather than just not helping?

They assume deliberate regulation and forced rest; both engage the interference and cascade mechanisms, which is predicted to worsen the state, not merely fail — a falsifiable prediction to test against experience, not a verdict on care. (5.3, 5.4, 10.1)

How could anyone actually prove this is a third topology and not just ASD plus ADHD?

By a subject-level test: comorbid signature values outside the parent range and a structured cluster found blind to labels would support it; values inside the range, or unstructured variance, would falsify it. (14)

This document is a theoretical model, not an established clinical or scientific consensus. It rests on a single neuroimaging study (Watanabe & Watanabe, 2023) conducted in children aged 5–13, combined with decades of personal observation and reflection. The neural findings provide a partial foundation; the sections on adult cognition, sensory processing, interoception, emotion, memory, sleep, and response to interventions are extrapolations that go beyond the available data. They are offered as an internally consistent description of one possible architecture, grounded in lived experience, but they have not been independently verified by controlled research.
